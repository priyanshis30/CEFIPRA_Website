Who we are

A bilateral centre, jointly run

CEFIPRA was set up in 1987 by the governments of India and France as a shared vehicle for advanced scientific and technological cooperation. On the Indian side it is backed by the Department of Science & Technology; on the French side, by the Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs.

Mandate

The Centre's job is to identify areas where Indian and French researchers can usefully work together, fund the resulting joint projects, and support the people-to-people exchange that keeps those partnerships alive — through seminars, workshops, mobility fellowships and industry-linked research.

How the Centre works

  • Academia–Academia collaboration through the Collaborative Scientific Research Programme, with a Thematic Call and a General Call issued each year.
  • Industry–Academia collaboration through the Industry-Academia R&D Programme, linking companies and research institutions in both countries.
  • Mobility and fellowship support for early-career researchers, including exchange fellowships between Indian and French institutions.
  • Seminars and workshops that bring scientists from both countries together to seed new joint projects.

People

CEFIPRA team

A small joint secretariat based in New Delhi manages the Centre's programmes, finances and communications on behalf of both governments.

Transparency

Right to Information (RTI)

As a body supported by the Government of India, CEFIPRA responds to information requests made under the Right to Information Act. Contact the secretariat for the current RTI officer and process.
