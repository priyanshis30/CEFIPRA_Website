Who we are

About The Centre

The Indo-French Centre for the Promotion of Advanced Research (CEFIPRA), also known by its French name IFCPAR, is a bilateral organisation set up by the Government of India and the Government of France to support collaborative research in advanced areas of science and technology.

Mandate

CEFIPRA's mandate is to promote collaborative research between India and France in cutting-edge science and technology. It does this not by running its own laboratories, but by identifying priority areas jointly with both governments, inviting proposals from Indian and French institutions, and funding and coordinating the projects, fellowships, seminars and industry partnerships that follow. The Centre is supported by the Department of Science & Technology, Government of India, and the Ministry for Europe & Foreign Affairs, Government of France.

How the idea began

The idea of a joint centre for Indo-French scientific collaboration was first discussed between the then Indian Prime Minister, Smt. Indira Gandhi, and the then French President, Mr. Valéry Giscard d'Estaing, in the late 1970s. President Giscard d'Estaing reiterated the suggestion in 1980, and the first formal proposal for the Centre was tabled at the second meeting of the Indo-French Joint Committee for Co-operation in Science and Technology, held in Paris in January 1982.

The Centre was registered on 16 April 1986 as a society under the Indian Societies Registration Act, 1860. Its Governing Body met for the first time in May 1987, and CEFIPRA became functional on 9 September 1987, when its first Director assumed charge.

What CEFIPRA funds

  • Scientific Collaborative Research Programme — academia-to-academia projects between Indian and French research institutions across a wide range of science and technology domains.
  • Industry-Academia Research & Development Programme (IARDP) — builds working links between industry and academia in both countries, co-funding applied R&D.
  • Mobility & fellowship support — including the Raman–Charpak Fellowship and the Programme for Women in Science (PROWIS), giving young researchers exposure to the working, social and cultural environment of the partner country.
  • Targeted Programmes — a platform for Indian and French national funding agencies to run joint calls in specific priority areas.
  • Innovation programmes (PPP mode) — where industry partners co-fund R&D alongside CEFIPRA in defined priority areas.
  • Seminars & workshops — thematic events that bring Indian and French scientists together and often seed new collaborative projects.

Current priority areas

Following its Governing Body meetings — the 37th in Paris (April 2024) and the 38th in New Delhi (February 2025) — CEFIPRA's Governing Board approved four priority research areas: Decarbonised Hydrogen, Applied Mathematics, Health (emerging infectious diseases, and metabolic & cardiovascular disease), and Marine Sciences. Recent initiatives launched under this agenda include the High-Impact Research Network Programme, PROWIS for women scientists, and the Indo-French Liver and Metabolic Disease Network (InFLiMeN), launched in New Delhi in July 2024.

CEFIPRA is co-chaired on the Indian and French sides, reflecting its status as a jointly governed, bi-national institution.

CEFIPRA Team

The Secretariat, based in New Delhi, is led by the Director and supported by programme officers on both the Indian and French sides who manage calls for proposals, project monitoring, fellowships and events throughout the year.
