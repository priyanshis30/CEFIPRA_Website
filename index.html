Who we are

Three decades of bilateral science

CEFIPRA — also known by its French name, Centre Franco-Indien pour la Promotion de la Recherche Avancée — is a joint body set up by the governments of India and France to grow scientific and technological cooperation between the two countries. It is funded on the Indian side by the Department of Science & Technology and on the French side by the Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs.

Rather than running its own laboratories, the Centre works as a funding and coordination body: it identifies promising areas of joint research, invites proposals from Indian and French institutions, and manages the projects, seminars, fellowships and industry partnerships that follow.
